    About Occupied territories of Georgia

    2008, after the Russian-Georgian war, the territories of Georgia occupied by Russia. These territories include the Autonomous Republic of Abkhazia and the former South Ossetia Autonomous District of Soviet Georgia.

    After the 2008 war and the subsequent Russian occupation of Abkhazia and South Ossetia, the Russian government, along with four other UN states, regards these territories as independent sovereign states: the Republic of Abkhazia and the Republic of South Ossetia. Before the Russian occupation, the unrecognized Republic of Abkhazia and the unrecognized Republic of South Ossetia did not fully control the territories they claimed. Russia has placed its military bases in Abkhazia and South Ossetia.

    Both territories, Abkhazia and South Ossetia, are universally recognized as an integral part of Georgia and together represent 20 percent of Georgia's internationally recognized territory. The "Law on Occupied Territories" adopted in 2008 criminalizes and prosecutes entry into Abkhazia and South Ossetia from Russian territory without special permission. The law of Georgia also allows any economic activity to be conducted there only in accordance with the legislation of Georgia.
